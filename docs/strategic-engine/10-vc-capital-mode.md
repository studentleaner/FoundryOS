# VC Capital Mode (VCM)

If Personal Strategic Mode (PSM) is the creative incubator, **VC Capital Mode (VCM)** is the cold, probabilistic capital allocator. Once an idea proves its structural dominance, the engine shifts into VCM to determine if the idea makes sense as a financial asset within a portfolio.

---

## 1. The Core Purpose of VCM

Venture returns rely on power-law math, not average successes. VCM translates the raw probabilities (MAV, CRI, CS) generated by the Monte Carlo simulator into rigid financial metrics. 

## 2. VCM Outputs & Impact Modeling

When unlocked, VCM produces an entirely different dashboard of outputs than PSM:

- **Exit Distribution Modeling:** Translates the idea's "5-Year Dominance Probability" into concrete venture-return multiples (e.g., 60% chance of 0x, 25% chance of 1-3x, 10% chance of 10x, 5% chance of 50x+).
- **Expected IRR Generation:** Factor in initial check size, expected equity ownership, and time horizon to calculate the probabilistic Internal Rate of Return across 1,000 Monte Carlo runs.
- **Portfolio Correlation Scaling:** Does this idea diversify the fund's risk, or amplify it? If an idea is highly correlated with 5 other SaaS tools in the portfolio, its allocation score is mathematically reduced.
- **Capital Allocation Sizing:** The engine outputs explicit sizing recommendations (e.g., *Core Bet*, *Diversifier*, *Optionality Bet*, *Avoid*).

---

## 3. The Mathematical Tone

VCM removes founder optimism entirely. It is designed to act as a **VC-Grade Capital Allocation Simulator**, protecting survival and maximizing upside asymmetry.

> *"The idea is structurally sound, but the Monte Carlo simulation exposes an extremely high correlation risk to cloud infrastructure pricing. If AWS raises costs, this idea's survival probability drops below 15%. Recommending a reduced 'Optionality Bet' check size to contain downside exposure."*

In VCM, a great product does not automatically equal a great investment.
