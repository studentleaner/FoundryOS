# The Rules of the AI-Era Game

Before writing code or allocating capital, you must accept the fundamental laws that govern the modern technology landscape. The **Strategic Intelligence Engine (SIE)** is built upon these unapologetic first principles. If an idea violates these laws, the engine will aggressively penalize it.

---

## 1. Commoditization is Accelerating
The cost to generate intelligent software is collapsing to zero. If your business model relies entirely on providing a thin UI over an LLM or an easily replicable workflow, your margin will compress.
- **The Rule:** You must explicitly calculate the **Commoditization Timeline**. If cloning your core value takes an incumbent less than 6 months, you do not have a company; you have a feature.

## 2. Infrastructure Dominance > Feature Layers
Applications live or die by user acquisition costs, but infrastructure generates immense switching costs.
- **The Rule:** The deeper you sit in the stack—orchestration, data storage, control planes—the higher your long-term defensive capability. The SIE explicitly rewards "Infrastructure Depth" significantly higher than "Application Breadth."

## 3. Distribution is Power (CAC is the Enemy)
Building it no longer means they will come. If you do not possess an asymmetric distribution advantage (a proprietary channel, extreme SEO velocity, or community leverage), you will bleed out in ad spend.
- **The Rule:** A brilliant product with high CAC pressure will be outlasted by a mediocre product with zero-CAC distribution.

## 4. Reaction Modeling Matters
Traditional business plans assume competitors will stand still while you steal their customers. They will not.
- **The Rule:** You must mathematically model the **Reaction Capability** of incumbents. Will they retaliate with a pricing war? Will they bundle your feature for free? The SIE simulates 24 months of competitive retaliation.

## 5. Power-Law Distribution Governs Returns
Venture capital is not about finding safe 2x returns. It is about capturing extreme outlier events that pay for entire portfolios of failure.
- **The Rule:** The SIE does not seek "average." It actively seeks out ideas with explosive upside asymmetry. If an idea has no mathematical path to outlier dominance, it is rejected by the VC Capital Mode (VCM).

## 6. Risk Asymmetry Beats TAM Optimism
Total Addressable Market (TAM) is a lagging illusion. A massive TAM often masks massive competition and high burn rates.
- **The Rule:** Focus on the **Upside Asymmetry Ratio** (Best Case Value / Worst Case Loss). It is better to attack a highly concentrated, defensible niche with an asymmetry ratio of 10:1 than a massive TAM with an asymmetry ratio of 1:1.

## 7. Survival Precedes Dominance
An idea cannot generate a 100x return if it runs out of cash in month 14.
- **The Rule:** Burn rates and fundamental unit economics are heavily modeled. The engine calculates an explicit **2-Year Survival Probability** before it even looks at 5-Year Dominance potential.
