# Cross-Perspective Analysis

An idea may look flawless from an engineering standpoint, but disastrous legally. The SIE forces every highly-scored automation gap through a violent **Cross-Perspective Analysis**—evaluating the thesis from the explicit viewpoints of all critical stakeholders.

## 1. The Founder's Lens
- **Speed to MVP:** Can this be validated within the 24-Hour execution window?
- **Differentiation Potential:** Is the Wedge strategy sharp enough to cut through incumbent noise?

## 2. The CTO's Lens
- **Integration Complexity:** Are there standardized APIs, or does this require scraping legacy mainframes?
- **Data Availability:** Do we have access to high signals, or is the data hopelessly siloed in enterprise networks?

## 3. The CFO's Lens
- **Measurable ROI:** Can we prove cost savings or revenue generation to a buyer within 3-6 months?
- **Cost Savings Timeline:** Is the payback period acceptable for enterprise procurement thresholds?

## 4. The Regulator's Lens
- **Legal Exposure:** Does this solution handle Protected Health Information (PHI) or biometric data?
- **Compliance Barriers:** Does it violate the EU AI Act or algorithmic fairness protocols?

## 5. The Employee & Operator's Lens
- **Resistance Level:** Will end-users boycott the tool because they fear job replacement?
- **Augmentation vs. Replacement:** Does it fundamentally empower the worker (Human-in-the-loop) or attempt to ruthlessly replace them?

## 6. The Investor's Lens
- **Scalability:** Will marginal costs approach zero as the user base expands?
- **Recurring Revenue:** Is this a one-time transactional fix, or an entrenched subscription engine?

*A venture thesis that fails in any two of these perspectives is flagged for immediate architectural revision or killed summarily.*
