# Structural Evaluation Engine (Module A)

The **Structural Evaluation Engine** is the first gate in the Strategic Intelligence Engine (SIE). Its sole purpose is to answer one question: 

> *"Is this idea structurally sound, or is it fundamentally fragile?"*

Before an idea is subjected to competitive threat analysis or Monte Carlo simulation, it must prove that its foundational mechanics work.

---

## 1. Market & Problem Depth
An idea that solves a shallow problem will ultimately fail to overcome customer acquisition friction.
- **Pain Urgency:** Is this a "hair-on-fire" problem or a "nice-to-have" novelty?
- **Frequency of Execution:** Does this problem occur daily, weekly, or annually? High-frequency problems naturally build retention.
- **Budget Ownership:** Which department or persona explicitly owns the budget to solve this problem?

## 2. Gap Detection & Validation
The engine detects if a genuine gap exists in the market or if the idea is simply "Feature Parity + Better UI."
- **Workflow Gaps:** Does the tool connect disconnected silos of enterprise workflow?
- **Cost Arbitrage:** Does the idea exploit a massive drop in AI compute costs that incumbents haven't adjusted to?
- **Friction Arbitrage:** Does it reduce a 10-step process to a 1-step process?

## 3. Economic Model & Feasibility
Ideas must make money without defying the laws of physics.
- **Unit Economics Base:** Calculating the baseline LTV (Lifetime Value) to CAC (Customer Acquisition Cost) ratio before scaling. 
- **Time-to-Value (TTV):** How long does it take for the user to experience the core value? High TTV correlates with extreme churn.
- **Capital Intensity:** How much capital is required to construct the initial infrastructure before revenue can be generated?

## 4. Moat Potential & Defensive Logic
What stops an incumbent from rebuilding this over a weekend?
- **Data Gravity:** Does the system accrue proprietary data with every transaction that makes it harder for the user to leave?
- **Workflow Integration:** How deeply embedded into the customer's daily operational stack does the tool get?
- **Infrastructure Position:** Is this an easily swappable application feature, or a foundational orchestration layer?

## 5. Risk & Fragility Exposure
The engine applies extreme pessimistic scrutiny to identify structural flaws.
- **Platform Dependency:** Does the idea rely entirely on OpenAI's API, Twitter's algorithm, or Apple's App Store goodwill? (High fragility risk).
- **Regulatory Surface Area:** Does the platform touch heavily regulated industries (Health, Finance) that require massive compliance overhead from Day 1?
- **Fraud Vectors:** Is the platform susceptible to automated abuse or chargeback devastation?

---

> [!NOTE]
> **Output:** The Structural Evaluation Engine produces an initial `Structural Score` out of 10. Ideas scoring below a 6.0 are immediately flagged with **Failed Integrity** and are kicked back to the user for repositioning.
