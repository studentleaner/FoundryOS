# Philosophy

The FoundryOS is built upon uncompromising principles. Without these fundamental philosophies, the platform degrades into just another checklist software.

## 1. Execution over Theory
Most founders think they are building when they are actually thinking. 

The FoundryOS rejects thought exercises. If you cannot extract metadata, deploy a repository, establish an event pipeline, and run a single GTM experiment, you do not have a startup. You have an opinion.

## 2. Evidence over Opinions
Interviews lie. Behavior doesn't. 
- Validation requires real friction. Unprompted complaints, email submissions, and financial transactions are acceptable signals. "Sounds cool" is not.

## 3. Systems over Ideas
Ideas are commoditized and cheap. The Execution Gap is where moats are built.
A startup is not a product; it is an interlocking set of systems (Architecture, Workflows, Observability, GTM). We build the system to test the idea, rather than building the idea directly.

## 4. Signals over Feelings
The Decision Engine exists to remove founder self-delusion. 
- You do not decide to scale because you "feel" like it's going well. You decide to scale because the cohort retention curves and AARRR metrics dictate it.
- Similarly, a "Kill" signal is not a failure. It is weeks, months, and dollars saved by respecting reality.

## 5. Constraint Drives Clarity
"Compress months of ambiguity into 24 hours of evidence."
By severely constraining the time window, the OS forces brutal prioritization. You can only ship the core feature. You can only test one marketing channel. The result is pure clarity.
