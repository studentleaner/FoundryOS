# Decision Matrix

The core responsibility of the VentureOS is to reach a binary conclusion. Startup ecosystems fail because they exist in a perpetual state of "maybe." The Decision Matrix enforces a resolution.

## How the Verdict is Reached

The Decision Matrix aggregates the inputs from the **Risk Engine** and the **Valuation Scenarios**. It applies strict, customizable gating logic based on the stage of the startup (Pre-Seed, Seed, Series A).

### The Three State Flags:

#### 1. Fund (Double-Down)
- **Condition:** Market Risk is low (CAC is sustainable, Activation is fast). Technical Risk is low (architecture is stable out of the box).
- **Action:** Triggers the generation of the Pitch Deck and signals the Founder to shift to the "Scale" module in the Builder Muscle.

#### 2. Hold (Pivot)
- **Condition:** Technical Risk is low (the team can execute and the product works), but Market Risk is high (no one cares, or the unit economics are upside down).
- **Action:** Generates an "Action Required" diagnostic report highlighting the exact failure (e.g., Conversion Drop-off at Checkout). The system pauses the project, reverting its state back to Stage 3 (MVP Architecture).

#### 3. Pass (Kill)
- **Condition:** Structural failure. The Cap Table is ruined, the iteration velocity is zero, and the metrics are non-existent over a defined timeframe (e.g., 90 days with zero organic traction).
- **Action:** Locks the project. Generates the post-mortem report and suggests infrastructure teardown routines to stop server burn.

## Investor Perspective
From an investor's seat, this matrix is the ultimate due diligence filter. It prevents capital deployment into "Hold" or "Pass" projects, regardless of the founder's pitch charisma.
