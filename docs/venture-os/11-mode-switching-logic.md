# Mode Switching Logic

The VentureOS contains two distinct perspectives: the formative **Personal Strategic Mode (PSM)** and the probabilistic **VC Capital Mode (VCM)**. 

To prevent premature rejection of early concepts, or the allocation of capital to structurally flawed ideas, the system relies on rigorous **Mode Switching Logic**.

---

## 1. The Trigger Thresholds

Ideas are ingested blindly into the PSM. They are not permitted to be viewed through the VC Capital Mode until they mathematically achieve defined structural strength thresholds during the Multi-Agent Debate.

An idea is automatically "Graduated" from PSM to VCM only if all the following conditions are met:

- **Differentiation Score ≥ 7.0:** The idea must prove it has a unique approach to solving the problem that is not simply a generic API wrapper.
- **Infrastructure Depth Score ≥ 6.0:** The idea cannot be purely an overlay application; it must demonstrate a path to owning data gravity or becoming a system of record.
- **Capital Efficiency Score ≥ 6.0:** The idea must have a mathematically viable path to scaling without requiring an infinite, unjustifiable cash burn.
- **Competitive Threat Score < 8.0:** The incumbent retaliation probability must be contained. If an incumbent can crush it immediately via bundled pricing, capital should not be allocated.

---

## 2. The Logic of the Gate

If an idea scores a `5.5` on Infrastructure Depth, the VCM remains locked. The engine alerts the user in PSM: 

> *"Graduation to VCM Locked: Idea acts as a shallow feature layer. To unlock VCM, you must reposition the product architecture to hook into the client's internal HR database, raising the Infrastructure Depth."*

By forcing ideas to stay in the PSM incubator until they earn the right to be evaluated for capital, the system simultaneously serves as:
1. **A Strategic Thinking Amplifier** (helping builders build better businesses).
2. **A Capital Quality Filter** (ensuring allocators only see highly-vetted assets).
