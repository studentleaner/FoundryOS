# Competitor Threat & Reaction Engine

It is a fatal mistake to assume incumbents will stand still while you steal their customers. The **Competitor Threat & Reaction Engine** forces a mathematical analysis of how dangerous your competition is, and exactly how they might destroy your margins.

---

## 1. Competitor Threat Scoring Framework (CTSF v1)

Every identified competitor is evaluated across 7 structural dimensions to calculate a composite `Threat Score (0-10)`.

- **Market Power Score (MPS):** Do they already own the audience?
- **Distribution Control Score (DCS):** Do they control the channel you rely on (e.g., Apple App Store, Google Search)?
- **Infrastructure Depth Score (IDS):** How deep into the enterprise stack are they?
- **Clone Speed Score (CSS):** *(The AI-Era Modifier)* How many weeks would it take an intern with an LLM to clone your core feature?
- **Switching Cost Lock-in Score (SLS):** How painful is it for their users to migrate to you?
- **Capital War Chest Score (CWS):** Can they afford to operate at a loss for 10 years to bleed you dry?
- **Regulatory Advantage Score (RAS):** Do they have massive compliance teams that act as a moat against startups?

> [!WARNING]
> **The AI-Era Threat Modifier:** If a competitor scores > 8 in **Clone Speed** and > 8 in **Distribution Control**, the threat is considered existential. They do not need to build a better product than you; they just need to build a "good enough" feature and distribute it to their existing audience.

---

## 2. Reaction Simulation Engine (RSE v1)

A high `Threat Score` alone is not enough to kill an idea; you must determine if the competitor will actually care.

The RSE simulates the probability and intensity of retaliation over a 24-month horizon. It calculates this using **Strategic Overlap Score (SOS):**

1. **Ignore/Dismiss (0-6 months):** The incumbent views the startup as a toy. High probability if SOS is low.
2. **Feature Bundling (6-18 months):** The incumbent clones the feature and gives it away for free to their existing users, instantly compressing the startup's margins.
3. **Aggressive Price War (18-24 months):** The incumbent deliberately lowers prices below cost to starve the startup of oxygen. High probability if SOS is high and capitalist threat is imminent.

### RSE Output Metrics:
- **Reaction Probability (RP):** The percentage likelihood of retaliation.
- **Estimated Margin Compression:** If retaliation occurs, what will happen to your pricing power? (e.g., "Expect a 40% margin compression by Month 14").
