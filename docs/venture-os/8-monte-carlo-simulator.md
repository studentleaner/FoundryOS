# Monte Carlo & Stress Simulator

With probability distributions assigned, the VentureOS executes a **Probabilistic Capital Allocation Engine (PCAE)**. It runs 1,000 simulated "futures" of the startup to observe how variability in adoption, competition, and capital intersect.

---

## 1. The 1000-Run Simulation

For each of the 1,000 runs, the system randomly assigns variable states based on the Probability Assignment Methodology (MAV, CRI, BRV, etc.).

For every simulation, it recomputes the `Final Composite Score` and determines if the startup survives 24 months, and what its 5-year dominance multiple looks like.

### Post-Simulation Output Profile:
- **Mean & Median Score:** The average expected outcome.
- **2-Year Survival Probability:** What percentage of the 1,000 runs resulted in the company running out of cash or being crushed by incumbents?
- **Expected IRR Distribution:** A mapping of venture-return multiples (0x, 1-3x, 5-10x, 20x+) based on the 5-year dominance probability.

---

## 2. Scenario Stress Testing

While Monte Carlo handles pure randomness, the system explicitly anchors 3 deterministic extreme scenarios.

### 🟢 Best Case Scenario
*- High adoption, weak competitor reaction, highly efficient capital burn, and slow AI commoditization.*
- **Output:** Sets the absolute maximum valuation potential.

### 🟡 Base Case Scenario
*- Moderate adoption, expected incumbent bundling, standard burn rates.*
- **Output:** The likeliest expected path.

### 🔴 Worst Case Scenario
*- Slow adoption, aggressive incumbent price wars, inefficient high burn, and rapid LLM-driven commoditization.*
- **Output:** Analyzes exact capital required to survive the worst case, and downside loss probability.

---

## 3. The Upside Asymmetry Ratio

The ultimate metric generated by the simulation engine is the **Upside Asymmetry Ratio**.

> **Formula:** `Upside Asymmetry = Best Case Value / Worst Case Loss`

VC Logic dictates that an idea with a mediocre base score of 6.5 may still be an incredible investment if the Asymmetry Ratio is > 5:1 (massive upside with contained downside). Conversely, an idea scoring an 8.0 may be rejected if its Upside Asymmetry is < 1:1, representing a "safe but unscalable" lifestyle business exposing capital to unrewarded risk.
