# Versioning & Evolution

Do not attempt to build the full institutional scope of the **VentureOS** on day one. Attempting to build IRR Monte Carlo simulations before the basic Structural Evaluation Engine is polished will result in catastrophic complexity. 

The system must iterate through deliberate maturity phases.

---

## Phase 1: VentureOS v1 (The Private Toolkit)
*Status: Current Target Architecture*

**Focus:** Single-user, deep, modular, and private. Functioning as a high-end "Strategic Thinking Amplifier." 
- **Core Features:** Idea Intake, Structural Evaluation, Competitor Threat Score, basic Multi-Agent Debate.
- **Goal:** Prove the engine's structural thinking is sharp, and that the Multi-Agent Debate produces genuine strategic insight.
- **Exclusion:** Heavy capital IRR logic is ignored.

## Phase 2: VentureOS v2 (The Capital Allocator)
*Status: Near-Term Roadmap*

**Focus:** Unlocking the **VC Capital Mode (VCM)** and probability mechanics.
- **Core Features:** Introduction of the Probability Assignment Engine, the 1000-run Monte Carlo Simulator, Upside Asymmetry calculation, and Exit Distribution modeling.
- **Goal:** Transition the tool from an advisory dashboard into a cold, probabilistic capital allocator capable of scoring venture-scale assets.

## Phase 3: VentureOS Institutional
*Status: Long-Term Vision*

**Focus:** Multi-tenant, API-driven, and portfolio-aware. 
- **Core Features:** Live competitor ingestion hooks, multi-portfolio correlation mapping, Bayesian self-correcting probabilities, and Sovereign risk modeling.
- **Goal:** Create a turnkey **Autonomous Investment Brain (AIB)** that can be licensed as the intelligence backbone for venture studios and institutional funds.
