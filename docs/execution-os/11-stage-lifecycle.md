# The 11-Stage Canonical Lifecycle

The Execution OS models the chaotic reality of startups into a predictable, deterministic 11-stage framework. Progress is gated by evidence; a project cannot advance without satisfying the explicit signals of its current stage.

## Stage 0: Ideation & Intent (Foundational)
- **Purpose:** Avoid building "interesting nonsense." Anchor on real pain.
- **Frameworks:** Problem–Pain–Frequency, Jobs To Be Done (JTBD), Founder–Market Fit.
- **Signals:** Users describe the pain without prompting; at least one paid-intent signal.

## Stage 1: Validation (Fast Experiments)
- **Purpose:** Validate behavior, not opinions. (Build–Measure–Learn).
- **Frameworks:** Lean experiments, Signal-based validation, Concierge/Wizard-of-Oz tests.
- **Signals:** Measurable email conversion, paid sign-ups, significant time-on-task.

## Stage 2: Business Design & Differentiation
- **Purpose:** Design a defensible model; define the value exchange.
- **Frameworks:** Business Model Canvas, Execution Gap Analysis, Switching Cost Analysis.
- **Signals:** Target initial channel LTV:CAC > 3, or a demonstrable path to it.

## Stage 3: MVP Spec / Architecture
- **Purpose:** Build the smallest system that forces real user behavior.
- **Frameworks:** Critical-Path Analysis, One-Core-Outcome Rule.
- **Signals:** One user completes the end-to-end operational job within defined SLAs.

## Stage 4: Product Metrics & Analytics (AARRR)
- **Purpose:** Measure behavior that drives decisions. 
- **Frameworks:** Pirate Metrics (Acquisition, Activation, Retention, Referral, Revenue), North Star Metric constraint.
- **Signals:** Clear conversion funnels; cohorts that improve after iteration.

## Stage 5: GTM / Marketing / SEO
- **Purpose:** Create repeatable, measurable acquisition machines.
- **Frameworks:** Single-Channel GTM, Intent-based Keyword Mapping (SEO as a product Strategy).
- **Signals:** One primary channel achieving predictable baseline conversion and positive ROAS/Organic scale.

## Stage 6: Retention & Product-Market Fit (PMF)
- **Purpose:** Prove users want to stay or pay. Growth without retention is fatal.
- **Frameworks:** Retention Curve Analysis, Cohort Analysis, Feedback-to-Feature Loops.
- **Signals:** D30 retention exceeds industry benchmarks; "Aha" moment instrumented.

## Stage 7: Business Operations & Revenue
- **Purpose:** Transition from experiments to scalable, repeatable revenue processes.
- **Frameworks:** OKRs, RACI mapping, SLA operations.
- **Signals:** Predictable YoY/MoM revenue growth, declining CAC, rising renewal rates.

## Stage 8: Scale (Infra, Org, Analytics)
- **Purpose:** Scale users, team, and systems reliably without structural collapse.
- **Frameworks:** 12-Factor App scaling, Bottleneck Theory, 18-Month Runway Modeling.
- **Signals:** Stable uptime under load, controlled cost-per-user, predictable sprint velocity.

## Stage 9: Governance, Security & Legal
- **Purpose:** Make the business historically defensible and legally investable.
- **Frameworks:** Operational Excellence, IP Assignment verification, Compliance posture (GDPR/SOC2).
- **Signals:** Clean corporate Cap Table, zero IP ambiguity, passed vulnerability checks.

## Stage 10: Fundraising & Investor Readiness
- **Purpose:** Raise capital on favorable terms powered by operational facts.
- **Frameworks:** Metric Memos > Slides; 3-Scenario Financial Modeling.
- **Signals:** Predictable acquisition, validated unit economics, high "Risk Resilience" grade from the Strategic Intelligence Engine.

## Stage 11: Exit Strategy & Liquidity Paths
- **Purpose:** Define how investors and founders eventually realize value.
- **Paths:** Strategic Acquisition, MBO (Management Buyout), Secondary/Growth Equity rounds, or IPO.
